Jalāl ad-Dīn Muhammad Rūmī "Rumi"

About

Jalāl ad-Dīn Muhammad Rūmī, more popularly known as Rumi, was a 13th century Persian Sunni Muslim poet, jurist, scholar, theologian and Sufi mystic.

Life

Rumi was born in Balkh, present day Afghanistan. He lived under the Persianate Seliug Sultanate of Rum, where he produced his work. He served as a jurist and a teacher.

Work

Rumi's poetry includes quatrains, odes, and six books of the Masnavi. The prose works are divided into The Discourses, The Letters, and the Seven Sermons.
